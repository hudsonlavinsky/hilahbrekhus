The Future of Journalism
Starts With Us

In a time of hyper-polarized media and eroding public trust, Journalism for Generations is a student-led initiative dedicated to preserving the craft of honest reporting and empowering young people to demand — and create — better news.

Our Mission

We believe journalism is not just an industry — it's a public good. When reporting is done with integrity, it holds power accountable, connects communities, and helps people make informed decisions about the world around them. But that foundation is fracturing. Misinformation spreads faster than corrections. Algorithms prioritize outrage over accuracy. And an entire generation is growing up unsure of what — or whom — to trust.

Journalism for Generations was founded to confront that reality head-on. Our mission is to ensure that future generations can not only interpret the news critically, but can eventually contribute to a well-functioning journalism industry themselves. We're building a space for media literacy, honest conversation, and the kind of slow, careful thinking that good reporting demands.

I

Understand

Learn how quality journalism works — the ethics, the process, and why it matters to a functioning democracy.

II

Recognize

Develop the tools to identify bias, misinformation, and the difference between reporting and opinion.

III

Contribute

Equip young people to create responsible, honest journalism in their own communities and beyond.

Welcome to Journalism for Generations

There was a moment during my junior year at Redwood High School when I realized something had shifted. A classmate shared a headline in our group chat, completely stripped of context, and within minutes people were arguing — not about the facts, but about which "side" the article was on. Nobody had actually read the piece. That moment stuck with me, and it became the seed for what you're reading right now.

Journalism for Generations exists because I believe my generation has a responsibility we didn't ask for but can't afford to ignore. We are growing up in an era where the line between news and opinion has been blurred almost beyond recognition, where algorithms decide what we see, and where trust in media is at historic lows. But instead of walking away from journalism, I think we need to walk toward it.

As a writer for The Redwood Bark, I've seen firsthand what real reporting looks like at its core. It's not glamorous. It means showing up to school board meetings. It means calling people back a second and third time. It means writing a story that might not get clicks but matters to the community. Student journalism taught me that the craft isn't about pushing an agenda. It's about asking honest questions and doing the difficult work of finding honest answers.

This site is my attempt to bring that ethos beyond the walls of one high school. Journalism for Generations is a space dedicated to three things: understanding how quality journalism works, recognizing when it doesn't, and equipping young people with the tools to both consume and create news responsibly. Through essays, interviews, and resources, I want to build a community of students and young readers who refuse to accept a broken media landscape as inevitable.

I'm not pretending to have all the answers. I'm eighteen, and I'm learning constantly. But I think that's actually the point. The future of journalism won't be shaped by pundits on cable news. It will be shaped by the people who grow up demanding better, and who are willing to do the work themselves.

If that sounds like something worth being part of, you're in the right place. Welcome aboard.
